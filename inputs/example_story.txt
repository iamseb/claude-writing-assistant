Chapter 1: The Discovery

Sarah had always been curious about the old house at the end of Maple Street. The Victorian mansion stood there, weathered and forgotten, with its paint peeling and windows boarded up. Nobody talked about who used to live there, and whenever Sarah asked her parents about it, they would change the subject quickly.

One rainy afternoon in October, Sarah decided she couldn't wait any longer. She had to know what secrets the house held. She grabbed her flashlight and headed out into the drizzle, her heart pounding with excitement and fear.

The front door was locked, but Sarah found a loose board covering one of the basement windows. She pried it away carefully and squeezed through the narrow opening, dropping down onto the dusty basement floor.

The basement was dark and musty, filled with old furniture covered in white sheets. Sarah's flashlight beam danced across the shadows as she explored. In the corner, she discovered an old trunk. When she opened it, she gasped.

Inside were dozens of photographs, all showing the same young woman. But what made Sarah's blood run cold was that the woman in every photograph looked exactly like her. Same face, same eyes, same smile. The photographs were dated from the 1920s, almost a century ago.

Sarah picked up one of the photographs with trembling hands. On the back, in faded ink, someone had written: "Emma - Forever Young."
